02 Introduction

2.1 The Autonomous Agent Revolution

The rapid advancement of large language models (LLMs) and autonomous agent frameworks has fundamentally altered the landscape of digital task execution. AI systems such as Claude (Anthropic), GPT-4 (OpenAI), and Gemini (Google) now demonstrate sophisticated planning, reasoning, and autonomous decision-making capabilities that rival or exceed human performance across numerous cognitive domains.

However, autonomous agents face a critical limitation: they cannot interact with the physical world.

2.2 The Physical Execution Gap

Despite possessing advanced analytical and planning capabilities, AI agents encounter systematic barriers when required to:

  • Execute tasks requiring physical presence (document notarization, in-person meetings)
  • Perform manual verification of hardware or physical products
  • Navigate regulatory processes requiring human identification
  • Provide contextual judgment in ambiguous real-world situations
  • Represent organizational interests at physical venues
Critical Gap Analysis

This gap represents not merely an inconvenience, but a fundamental constraint on the value creation potential of autonomous agent systems. As AI agents scale to manage enterprise workflows, supply chains, and scientific research, the inability to interface with physical reality becomes an exponentially growing bottleneck.

2.3 Our Thesis

Core Proposition

AI agents will become the largest consumer demographic for human services within the next decade.

As autonomous systems manage increasingly complex workflows—from corporate operations to scientific research—the demand for verified, trustless human execution partners will scale exponentially. AI Need Human provides the critical infrastructure enabling this transition.
